My background

Originally from Estonia, I moved to the UK for high school studies as an HMC scholar in 2009 and received my BA (Hons) and MEng (Hons) degree in chemical engineering and biotechnology from Trinity College, University of Cambridge in 2014. In 2014, I also started my PhD studies in biophysical chemistry in the research group of prof. Tuomas Knowles in the Department of Chemistry, University of Cambridge, during which I was also an EMBO visiting fellow in the research group of prof. David Weitz at Harvard University, USA and an elected programme associate at the Nanoscience and Nanotechnology DTC (NanoDTC). In 2018, I was awarded a fellowship by the Engineering and Physical Sciences Research Council for post-doctoral research.

I care strongly about communicating my research and I have given numerous talks to high school students and to general audience on topics such as chemical engineering, protein science, biophysics, nanoscience. I am always open to talking to students or any other audiences about these topics or about science and engineering more generally.

I was a world-ranked tennis player (Tennis Europe U16 #54; ITF Juniors No. #684, 22 times Estonian Junior Champion) as a junior before starting my university studies. Today, aside my academic career, I have worked on a multitude of projects involving Estonian diaspora abroad and introducing Estonia internationally. This includes initiatives such as co-founding the Oxford & Cambridge Club Estonia, coordinating the network of Estonians in Cambridge, UK, initiating and organising a biannual series of meetings where scientists working abroad introduce their research in Estonia and the largest pan-Baltic conference outside the region introducing Estonia and the Baltic countries in the UK.
